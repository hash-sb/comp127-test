## The rules of pairing

You are now pair programming! Here are the rules:

1. **Multiple people, one keyboard.**  When pairing in person, programmers will actually physically hand the keyboard back and forth (i.e. swap being the driver/navigator).

2. **There are two roles: driver and navigator.**  The **driver**  is the person with control of the keyboard. Their job is to think about details. The **navigator**  is the person who is watching. Their job is to stay actively engaged in what the driver is doing while thinking about the big picture.

    Driver: _â€œWhat should the next line of code do? What should I type? What is the syntax to use? What file or line of code has that thing weâ€™re looking for?â€_

    Navigator: _â€œWhat is our goal? What do the instructions say? Does what weâ€™re doing make sense? Are we getting ourselves lost? Should we be asking for help?â€_

3. **Switch roles often.**  If you are starting to dictate what to type, you should be the driver. If you are thinking out a big picture thatâ€™s coming faster than you can type, you should be the navigator. The appropriate social form is, â€œMay I drive?â€ or â€œWould you like to drive?â€ Then hand the keyboard or laptop over.
